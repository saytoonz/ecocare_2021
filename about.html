EcoCare Elevator Pitch

EcoCare Waste Initiative is an environmental social enterprise born in Goaso, Ahafo, Ghana. We deal in waste collection, cleaning and recycling businesses, and payback incentives for waste segregation – to fight improper waste disposal. Our goal is to continue to solve problems that affect people and their environments, land and natural resources depletion. EcoCare’s key SDG focus are on SDG 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation), SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production) and SDG 13 (Climate Action).

Our Vision & Mission

Our vision:
Our vision in the long term therefore is to become a world class environmental company solving global sustainability and ecological problems and inspiring African excellence.


Our mission:
EcoCare Waste Initiative is an environmental social enterprise born more with the mission to develop innovative technologies for managing wastes and protecting environments for people and communities.

Our Goal & Objectives

Our goal in the short to medium term is to create a sustainable solution to the growing plastic and organic waste menace, alternatives to landfills and chemical fertilizer while providing decent job opportunities in the environmental and sanitation sectors in our communities. Our goal in the long term will be to scale up into a big social enterprise that will produce and supply Ghanaians with green energy (like solar, biogas and other biofuels) and shift Ghana's dependence on timber for furniture onto recycled plastic. Key strategies we use are: to Design low-tech waste recycling ideas; Inspire people with our ideas, to challenge the wasteful paradigm; and to Promote recycled products "Don't Waste Your Waste," is our commitment motto to environmental excellence integrated with a business approach to sustainability.


Our objectives:

a. To develop low-tech reuse and recycling strategies and products from all recyclable plastic, paper and organic waste

b. To educate the world to challenge the wasteful paradigm

c. To earn a sustainable economic, social and ecological value in the Ghanaian waste management sector

d. To reduce the world's waste menace (especially plastic and paper) drastically.

Problems solved

i. Indiscriminate disposal of municipal and industrial waste

ii. Informality in the waste management industry

iii. Increased pressure on landfills

iv. Poor environmental sanitation and health.

v. Climate change.

EcoCare Culture

EcoCare strives to hire the best and we value integrity, excellence, respect, inclusion, and collaboration. What is special about EcoCare, however, is how much we:
1. work together as best friends
2. have fun together whenever we can
3. freely challenge ourselves and the status quo
4. encourage independent decision-making by employees
5. hold everyone on staff accountable to the vision

Our core philosophy is people and their environments over profits. More specifically, we are a community of environmental change makers working together as The EcoCarers. With this approach, we are a more flexible, fun, creative, collaborative and successful organization.

“At EcoCare we believe that our brand is our business philosophy and oour culture, more than our logo and colours.” JACKSON NYARKO (Co-Founder & Director)

It’s easy to write admirable values; it’s harder to live them. Thus to be confident and able to live by this seemingly daunting values of EcoCare – you will need courage. In describing courage we say, “You question actions inconsistent with our values ‐ including your own actions and actions of everyone else. And you are willing to go the extra mile to find more workable solutions and actions.” We want everyone to help each other live the values and hold each other responsible for being role models. It is a continuous aspirational stretch. This will make EcoCare the world class Environment Company and the African excellence we envisions.

Our Business Phylosophy

Planet earth (Nature in general) is threatened by economic systems that value short term profits and relentless growth. At EcoCare we want profits but we seek only sustainable growth. Our values come first, even if it that means sacrificing sales. We will strive to always minimise our environmental impact in all our value chains, and will atone for the damage we do – by giving either 1% of sales or 10% of profits to environmental causes – whichever is greater. So we will always look at the long term. Only by acting as if we will be in business for 100 years can we grow suitably.
Every ecosystem has a breaking point or a carrying capacity; you can only push them to the limits but not over. It’s the same thing for business. We must know our limits. Companies that tried to have it all only died faster. We will teach the world that there’s another way to do business.

How Sustainable is the whole EcoCare Model? *

We hope to use the following Social, Environmental and Economic (TBL/PPP) approaches to make our project sustainable:

1. In-depth Research and development activities at our "EcoCare Recycling Valley"

2. Run a website and an information desk to educate the world on eco-friendly waste management while promoting recycled products

3. Radio shows and community campaigns on how to manage waste the eco-friendly way, while marketing our recycled products and ideas

4. Waste collection business to make money to pay for staff and volunteers allowances and other expenses

5. Production and sales of recycled products to make economic profits

6. Organize training workshops and exhibition conferences aimed at developing creative recycling and manufacturing ideas in young people, while generating funds (IGF).

7. Scaling up in future to include production of green energy like biogas; and manufacturing of plastic fence posts
